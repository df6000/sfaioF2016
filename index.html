Interactive Art Fall 2016

Course Objectives

This course introduces students to the concepts, tools and workflows involved in creating digital works of art, with a focus on interactivity and “expanded” tools. We’ll begin by exploring what is capable using tools such as Adobe Creative Suite, and other oﬀ the shelf solutions, but we will also learn the underlying properties of digital media with an eye towards getting “under the hood” to make deeper and more creative manipulations, mostly using the javascript port of Processing, P5.js. The course will introduce the language of game design, social practices, user experience, and interaction design, and explore concepts in art and art history through the lens of the digital. Students will learn to translate physical gestures and environmental data into responsive experiences, and explore the creation of unique interfaces using sensors, switches and Arduino.

Projects

The primary work of this course will be done within the context of four major projects, each of which explores a different mode of interactivity. Although we will mostly be discussing interactive art as a digital medium with some analog antecedents, all of these projects may be done in any medium or combination of mediums. If a sculpture or a deck of cards is more appropriate than a video game for communicating your idea, then by all means go for it!

p1: performative: Click! Build an interface for navigating content.

p2: reformulation: Create a tool that lets people modify an 'original' artwork.

p3: collaboration: Create a space/tool/game/artwork that encourages collaboration.

p4: immersion: Create an immersive, interactive artwork.

Grading

Come to class! Participate! Do the readings. Complete assignments and projects as given, and your grade will take care of itself. Your grade will be based on the following:

35% attendance, class participation

15% midterm

25% final project

25% smaller assignments

Attendance and Participation at critiques is essential.
